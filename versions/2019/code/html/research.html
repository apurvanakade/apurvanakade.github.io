Research

I work in the area of algebraic topology and homotopy theory. I have been working on the following projects.

Projects

Equivariant Embedding Calculus

This project is a continuation of my PhD Thesis. The goal is to extend embedding calculus to an equivariant setting for manifolds with group actions.

2-groups in Equivariant Elliptic Cohomology

I got serendipitously involved in this project thanks to an MRC Workshop. The goal of this project is to find a 2-categorical interpretation of some of naturally occuring objects (Drinfield double of a group) in mathematical physics and extend them using ideas from Equivariant Elliptic Cohomology.

An application of the h-principle to Manifold Calculus.

This was my PhD thesis, in which I applied homotopy theoretic techniques to study problems for differential geometry. Here is the paper (submitted to Journal of Homotopy and Related Structures) based on the Thesis (Updated on: May 07, 2019). Here are some notes [1], [2] from the talks I gave on this topic.

HoTT and Proof-checking using computers

This is one of the new areas that I am getting involved in thanks to being at UWO. I hope to write more on this soon.

Protein folding using Markov chains

During my undergrad, I briefly worked on a project involving some protein folding models using basic linear algebra. Information Processing Letters, IPL4801.
