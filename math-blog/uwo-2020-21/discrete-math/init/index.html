Let's do this
Filed under discrete math, August 26, 2020.

teaching is a complex process. where i’m using the word complex in the same way that atul gawande uses in the checklist manifesto. there are so a lot of moving parts from planning to execution and a lot of room for error. sometimes small changes have large effects and sometimes a huge effort goes to complete waste.

the way university math education currently works is that it is taken as self-evident that someone who is good at math is going to excel at teaching. this is how i started teaching. i just improvised. fortunately, i am some training (and dare i say talent) at doing improv comedy so things have worked out well for me so far. by design, improv comedy works only works on a small scale. more nuanced approaches toward comedy, like stand-up or sketch, require a lot of planning and practice.

the same is true with teaching. you can improvise in the beginning but if you want to be good at it you have to plan, plan and plan. i have been planning quite a lot of courses for the past few years on various scales. but i am realizing that i need to experiment, document, reflect, and analyze more systematically.

enter this blog. i recently started a math-ed wiki to dump resources and now i’m starting this blog to dump my ideas, strategies, observations, and results.
